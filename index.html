Discover Henry W. Coe State Park

This large State Park is located in the hills right next to Pacheco Pass. It sits in ranching country, in between the Silicon Valley and the San Joaquin Valley in California.

A Primitive Wilderness Park

Thickly wooded gullies, deep canyons and wide open, unforested vistas and hundreds of miles of trail cover this mosty backcountry wilderness park.

Pick a loop to hike

Day hikers, back-country trekkers, equestrians and mountain bikers park find their trail, and so many trails criss-cross the park, never does one have to do the same hike twice.

The Best Time to Visit

Winter is wet, summer is hot and autumn is pleasant but dry. Some like it that way. But the best time of all to visit Coe is spring, after the rains end and before the heat arrives. That's time of the wildflowers throughout the inland foothill country of California.

Get In On The Social Experience

There is no shortage of hiking, mountain biking and horse riding groups in Central California. It's one of the best things about living in this state.

This park is run by volunteers

This wonderful park is lavished upon by weekend warriors who maintain the trail system and other park assets. Join them to spend a day giving back.
